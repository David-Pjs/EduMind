# EduMind — AI Tutor for Africa & India
### Bincom Hac/<athon 5.0 | "Hacking genAI" | 24-hour build

---

## The Problem

- In Nigeria, Ghana, Kenya, and India — classrooms have **50–100 students per teacher**
- Most families **cannot afford private tutors**
- Students are studying from **poor-quality printed textbooks** in their **second or third language**
- Power cuts and low bandwidth are everyday realities
- **300 million+ students** have almost zero access to quality 1-on-1 educational support

---

## Our Solution: EduMind

> *"The tutor every student deserves but few can access — now free, 24/7, and in their pocket."*

EduMind is a **generative AI tutoring chat app** powered by Ai. A student opens it on any phone or laptop, asks a question in any way they can — voice, text, or a photo of their textbook — and gets a clear, patient, grade-appropriate explanation instantly.

---

## How It Works

| Feature | What it does |
|---|---|
| **8 Subjects** | Math, Science, English, History, Physics, Biology, Chemistry, General |
| **5 Grade Levels** | Primary 1 → University (adapts AI language complexity automatically) |
| **Voice Input** | Hold the mic button and speak your question (Web Speech API) |
| **Text-to-Speech** | The AI reads its answer aloud — great for low-literacy or tired students |
| **Camera / Image Upload** | Point at your textbook or homework — AI reads and explains it |
| **Practice Quiz Mode** | Tap "🧠 Quiz Me" → get a 3-question multiple-choice quiz with answers, tailored to your subject and grade, using real Nigerian/Ghanaian/Kenyan/Indian curriculum |
| **Progress Tracking** | Questions asked, answer streak, sessions saved locally |
| **PWA / Installable** | Works like an app on Android & iOS — no app store needed |
| **Offline-friendly design** | Minimal data usage, concise responses, no bloat |

---


## The AI System Prompt (what makes it special)

The AI is instructed to always:
1. Adapt language difficulty exactly to the student's grade level
2. Use simple English first, then build complexity
3. Analyze photos of textbooks and handwritten notes carefully
4. End every response with a follow-up question OR motivational line
5. Use numbered steps and bullet points for visual clarity
6. Celebrate effort — "make this student feel SEEN" (invisible in large classes)
7. Suggest the next topic to keep the learning journey going

## Why This Wins

- **Real impact story** — addresses a documented 300M+ student crisis
- **Multimodal** — text, voice, AND vision in one app
- **Zero infrastructure cost** — pure frontend, no backend, no database
- **Accessible** — works on any phone, installable, low data
- **Polished** — full dark-theme UI, mobile responsive, animations, quiz mode
- **Africa/India specific** — curriculum references Nigeria, Ghana, Kenya, India by name in the AI prompt
